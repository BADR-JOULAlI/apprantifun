The Lion and the Mouse

Once upon a time, in a lush green forest, there lived a mighty lion. One day, while he was sleeping peacefully under a large tree, a tiny mouse accidentally ran across his nose.

The lion woke up with a loud roar! He caught the little mouse between his powerful paws.

"Please let me go, mighty king of the forest!" squeaked the mouse. "I'm so sorry for disturbing your sleep. If you spare me, I promise I'll help you someday!"

The lion found this very amusing. How could such a tiny creature ever help him? But he was feeling generous that day, so he let the mouse go.

A few days later, the lion was walking through the forest when he suddenly got caught in a hunter's net! He struggled and roared, but couldn't break free.

The little mouse heard the lion's roars and quickly ran to help.

The mouse began gnawing at the ropes with his sharp teeth. It took some time, but finally, the mouse managed to create a hole big enough for the lion to break free.

"Thank you, little friend," said the lion. "I was wrong to laugh at your offer of help. Even the smallest friend can be a great ally."

Moral of the Story:

A kindness is never wasted, and we should never underestimate others based on their size or appearance.
